PROBLEM OVERVIEW

We chose to confront the issue of poor feedback mechanisms in dining halls and cafeterias. Dining hall customers find it difficult to leave feedback on meals and service, and dining hall staff often do not know the issues that customers are having with the services the staff provide. Customers resort to either leaving no feedback or feel awkward when asking for (generally ineffective) comment cards directly from the staff they are looking to criticize. When the staff does receive feedback it is often a challenge to implement changes and provide solid, observable results. This results in low motivation to give feedback to dining halls.

We chose to confront the dining hall process in proactive way; instead of focusing on the feedback a customer might give about the meals they have eaten after the fact, our designâ€™s main purpose is to enable the user to have a preemptive way of deciding what foods will be served at the dining hall daily, thus providing the user with an immediate and observable change in the dining hall procedure as well as reducing negative feedback after the fact due to unwanted and unliked meals. We also implemented a feature so a user may give feedback retroactively on past meals. This lowers the barrier of entry and awkwardness for giving criticism to the meals the dining hall produces. Finally, we included a menu feature, so that users may easily know what will be in the dining hall besides what they have chosen for themselves.
